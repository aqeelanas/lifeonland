Promote Access to Genetic Resources

Access to genetic resources is vital for sustainable development,conservation of biodiversity, and the well-being of human societies.By ensuring equitable access to these resources, we can promote research and innovation in agriculture, medicine, and environmental conservation. Genetic resources provide the raw material for breeding programs that develop resilient crop varieties, enhance food security, and adapt to changing climate conditions. Moreover, they are crucial for discovering new medicines and biotechnological applications. Promoting access involves implementing fair policies that recognize the contributions of indigenous communities,ensuring benefit-sharing, and supporting international cooperation.


Fair Sharing of the Benefits

The fair sharing of benefits derived from the use of genetic resources is essential for fostering trust and cooperation among countries and communities. This principle ensures that those who contribute to the preservation and discovery of these resources, particularly indigenous peoples and local communities, receive a fair share of the benefits, whether they are monetary or non-monetary. Benefit-sharing can include financial compensation, technology transfer, capacity building, and access to research findings.

Strategies for Preventing the Spread of Invasive Alien Species on Land

  • Preventing the spread of invasive alien species on land is crucial for protecting native biodiversity, ecosystem stability, and agricultural productivity.
  • Invasive species can outcompete, prey on, or introduce diseases to native plants and animals, leading to significant ecological and economic damage.
  • Effective prevention strategies include implementing stringent biosecurity measures, promoting public awareness and education, and establishing early detection and rapid response systems.
  • By regulating and monitoring the introduction of non-native species, enforcing quarantine protocols, and promoting responsible agricultural and landscaping practices, we can reduce the risk of invasions.
  • Habitat restoration and research on invasive species' impacts and management can help mitigate their effects.

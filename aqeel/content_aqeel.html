CONSERVE AND RESTORE TERRESTRIAL AND FRESHWATER ECOSYSTEMS

The goal to "Conserve and Restore Terrestrial and Freshwater Ecosystems" focuses on preserving and improving the health of land and water environments to ensure they continue providing essential services to humanity and the planet. By 2020, this objective aimed to protect forests, wetlands, mountains, and drylands from degradation and destruction, thereby maintaining biodiversity and ensuring the resilience and functionality of ecosystems. It also emphasized the importance of actively restoring damaged ecosystems to a healthy state through rehabilitation efforts, such as reforestation and wetland restoration, to recover their ecological integrity and services. Furthermore, this goal aligned with international obligations, underscoring the global commitment to sustainable land and water management practices that support both environmental and human well-being.

END DESERTIFICATION AND RESTORE DEGRADED LAND

The goal to "End Desertification and Restore Degraded Land" by 2030 represents a global commitment to combating the deterioration of land and soil caused by desertification, drought, and floods. The objective extends beyond mere conservation to actively restoring degraded landscapes, particularly those affected by environmental stressors. This initiative aims to achieve a "land degradation-neutral world," where the balance between land degradation and restoration efforts results in a stable or improved land quality globally. To reach this goal, strategies include implementing sustainable land management practices, promoting reforestation and afforestation, restoring degraded ecosystems, and enhancing soil conservation techniques. By restoring degraded land and mitigating the impact of desertification and drought, societies can protect biodiversity, improve water resources, enhance food security, and build resilience against climate change impacts. International cooperation and adherence to agreements like the UN Convention to Combat Desertification are crucial in achieving these ambitious targets, ensuring that future generations inherit healthy and productive land ecosystems.

ENSURE CONSERVATION OF MOUNTAIN ECOSYSTEMS

The goal to "Ensure Conservation of Mountain Ecosystems" by 2030 focuses on preserving the unique and diverse environments found in mountainous regions. This includes protecting the biodiversity within these ecosystems to maintain their health and resilience. Mountain ecosystems are vital as they provide numerous essential benefits such as water resources, habitat for unique species, and opportunities for tourism and recreation. Conserving these areas is crucial for sustainable development because it ensures the continued availability of these resources and benefits for future generations. The initiative calls for effective management practices and policies that prioritize the protection of mountain landscapes, promote sustainable land use, and address the impacts of climate change and human activities on these fragile environments. By safeguarding mountain ecosystems, we can enhance their ability to support the livelihoods of millions of people who depend on them and contribute to global ecological balance.
